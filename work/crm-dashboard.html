B2B SAAS · CASE STUDY · 2023

Nexus CRM — Sales &
Operations Dashboard

A 200-person sales team operating across five disconnected tools, no single view of pipeline health, and an onboarding process that took three weeks. I redesigned the core CRM dashboard — consolidating data, establishing a component system, and cutting the time to act on critical account signals.

Project type
B2B SaaS · Internal Tool
Role
Lead Product Designer
Duration
4 months · 2023
Scope
UX Research UI Design Design System Prototyping
[ Nexus CRM — main dashboard overview ]
01 · Context

Nexus serves mid-market B2B companies with sales teams managing complex, multi-stakeholder deals. Sales reps were context-switching between a legacy CRM, a separate analytics platform, a task manager, email, and spreadsheets — stitching together a picture of each account manually, multiple times per day.

The platform had grown through feature additions rather than coherent design — each module built by a different team at a different time. The result was a fragmented experience that slowed reps down and made onboarding new hires unnecessarily painful.

My mandate was to redesign the core dashboard surface — establishing a unified information architecture, a reusable component system, and a set of interaction patterns that would carry forward into future modules.

02 · Research & Discovery

Before touching any interface, I spent two weeks in discovery: shadowing sessions with 8 sales reps, 4 team leads, and 2 ops managers. The pattern was clear — the tool wasn't the problem, the data fragmentation was.

FINDING 01
5 tools, 1 workflow
Reps averaged 47 tab switches per day. Pipeline data lived in the CRM, but context — emails, notes, tasks — lived everywhere else.
FINDING 02
No prioritisation signal
The dashboard showed everything equally. Reps had no way to see which accounts needed attention today without manually reviewing each record.
FINDING 03
Activity data was invisible
Email opens, call logs, and meeting history were stored but buried in sub-pages. Reps routinely missed signals that a deal had gone cold.
FINDING 04
3-week onboarding
New hires needed three weeks to become independently productive. Navigation logic was inconsistent; there was no clear mental model to latch onto.
03 · Outcomes

Validated through usability testing with 12 participants and a 6-week beta with a pilot cohort of 40 reps.

40%
Reduction in time to locate and act on at-risk account signals
60%
Reduction in context-switching (tab switches per session) in usability testing
1wk
Onboarding time cut from 3 weeks to 1 week for new sales hires
04 · What I Built
01 / 04
Unified Dashboard with Priority Feed
The core insight from research: reps didn't need more data, they needed the right data surfaced at the right moment. I designed a priority-first home view — an intelligent activity feed that surfaces accounts with recent signals (email opens, deal stage changes, overdue tasks) at the top, pushing inactive records down. Reps arrive knowing exactly where to start.

02 / 04
Account 360° View
The scattered-tools problem was solved by collapsing external context into the account record itself. The 360° view pulls timeline data (emails, calls, meetings, notes), deal stage, open tasks, and stakeholder map into a single scannable layout. One page replaces five tools. The information hierarchy was designed around the rep's mental model — "What's happened, what's next, who's involved."

03 / 04
Pipeline Kanban & Forecasting
Team leads needed a fast way to review pipeline health across their whole team without opening individual records. I designed a compact pipeline kanban — colour-coded by deal health, with inline forecast data and drag-to-stage functionality. A sidebar panel lets managers drill into any deal without losing their pipeline view context.

04 / 04
CRM Component System
Every pattern designed for this dashboard was codified into a shared component library — data tables, status chips, activity cards, inline editing states, empty states, and loading skeletons. This system became the foundation for the platform's next two modules (Reporting and Settings), making future design work significantly faster and keeping the product visually consistent as it scaled.
